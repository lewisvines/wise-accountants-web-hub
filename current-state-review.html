00 — Method

The baseline is the argument

The fastest way to win buy-in for a redesign is not to show the shiny demo first — it's to show, plainly and fairly, what a prospect sees today, and let the gap speak. Each page below is reviewed on its own terms: what it's doing well, where it falls short of the WiSE standard, and what that means commercially.

Each market is then placed on four buy-in dimensions: regulatory urgency (how hard the clock is ticking), page gap (distance from the standard), strategic stakes (what the channel is worth), and effort to close. Together they produce a defensible priority order — not a matter of taste, but of where the gap and the stakes are largest at once.

i

A note on fairness

These are real, working pages built by teams under real constraints. The point is not that the work is poor — it's that the market changed underneath it. The regulatory cliff is new; the pages largely predate it. That's the gap worth funding.

01 — France
FR
France · Experts-comptablesThe hard-cliff market — and the widest gap
PriorityHighest
Reviewed: sage.com/fr-fr/experts-comptables/produits/ and the experts-comptables product set.

What the page is today

A long product catalogue — roughly eighteen products grouped by verb (Automatiser, Communiquer, Produire, Conseiller): Génération Experts Connect, Sage Service Paie, Supervizor, Conciliator Expert, Beeye, transfertBANQUE, Waibi, Smartisi, MyPassPro, Compta Collaborative, LinkUp Experts, and more — followed by a generic article carousel and standard "be called back / contact Sage" CTAs.

What works

  • Genuine product depth — the portfolio breadth is real
  • Strong institutional proof points (CNOEC / CJEC partnerships) exist elsewhere on the site
  • Génération Experts is correctly positioned as the production core

The gap vs the standard

  • No regulatory story at all — the September 2026 e-invoicing cliff, the single biggest thing on every French accountant's mind, is absent
  • No mention of AKAO, the Approved Platform (PA), the PPF, or Sage's compliance readiness
  • Product-led, not customer-led — opens on features, not the client's reality
  • Fragmented, legacy naming ("anciennement Coala"; Conciliator, Supervizor, Waibi, Smartisi…) with no "one platform" story
  • Stale content — the article carousel still references the France 2023 Rugby World Cup
  • No pricing, no roadmap, no AI narrative, generic CTAs
Why it's first: France has the clearest, hardest regulatory deadline of the three and the page that says least about it. A prospect arriving with "what do I do about September 2026?" finds a product list and a two-year-old rugby article. The gap between market urgency and page content is at its maximum here — which is exactly where a redesign returns the most.
02 — Spain
ES
Spain · Asesorías y DespachosThe most mature page — needs sharpening, not rebuilding
PriorityMedium
Reviewed: sage.com/es-es/software-asesorias-y-despachos/, the Despachos and SfA product pages, and the asesorías blog.

What the page is today

A value-led landing page — "software that takes your firm beyond tax and accounting," with "Automatización y Crecimiento" framing — anchored on Sage Despachos Connected and the ContaPlus heritage, with Sage for Accountants (two business models) present. Crucially, the regulatory hook is surfaced: product copy references adapting to "las nuevas normas de facturación como Verifactu o factura electrónica," and the blog is actively maintained (2026 fiscal calendar, "VeriFactu Ready," 2026 tax updates).

What works

  • Regulatory reality is present — VeriFactu and factura electrónica appear, not absent as in FR
  • Sage for Accountants is surfaced, with a clear two-model choice
  • Content engine is current and credible — active 2026 advisory content
  • Value-led framing ("beyond tax and accounting") is closer to customer-voice

The gap vs the standard

  • The regulatory hook is buried in product copy, not leading the page as the wedge
  • No "two clocks, one platform" clarity — VeriFactu and Crea y Crece aren't framed as a single Sage answer
  • Legacy naming persists (Despachos Connected, ContaPlus) with overlapping lines
  • Still suite/product-structured rather than narrative (clock → platform → roadmap → AI → pricing)
Why it's medium: Spain is closest to the standard already — the raw materials (regulatory hook, SfA, current content) are on the page. The work is editorial, not foundational: lead with the dual-clock, consolidate the naming, and lift SfA above the fold. High return for relatively low effort.
03 — Portugal
PT
Portugal · ContabilistasGood flagship, fragmented estate, dated terminology
PriorityMedium
Reviewed: sage.com/pt-pt/contabilistas/sage-for-accountants/ (+ pricing), and the Sage One / Sage 50 / Sage 100 Contabilidade lines.

What the page is today

The Sage for Accountants flagship is decent: an integrated accounting / payroll / asset / practice-management proposition, with a real pricing page and a desktop-vs-cloud choice. Compliance is grounded correctly in Portugal's actual reality — SVAT certification (the AT validation seal), SAF-T (PT), and IES/DA — rather than a borrowed "deadline" narrative. But around it sits a fragmented legacy estate (Sage One, Sage 50 Contabilidade, Sage 100 Contabilidade) with overlapping positioning.

What works

  • SfA flagship is clear, with transparent pricing and a model choice
  • Compliance is correctly grounded in PT reality (SVAT, SAF-T, IES/DA)
  • Active advisory blog content for contabilistas

The gap vs the standard

  • Fragmented portfolio — SfA competes with Sage One, Sage 50 and Sage 100 for the same buyer
  • Dated terminology — "Sage One TOC" still uses the deprecated "TOC" label (the profession is now Contabilista Certificado)
  • Sage's own PT content uses "migrar / migração" — the banned change word
  • No single narrative arc; product pages rather than a customer journey
Why it's medium (and a useful mirror): the real PT page is actually better grounded in local regulation than our own PT demo, which over-rotated to a "2027 cliff." The work here is consolidation and terminology — collapse the estate behind SfA, retire "TOC," and remove "migração" — more than a ground-up rebuild.

Four dimensions, scored High / Medium / Low. Priority is highest where urgency and page-gap are both high — i.e. where the market is moving fastest and the page is least ready.

The one-line case

France is the only market where the highest regulatory urgency meets the widest page gap. Updating it first protects the flagship WiSE market at the exact moment its prospects are actively searching for answers the current page doesn't give.

05 — Recommendation

A sequenced, low-risk path

Each step compounds: the France rebuild produces the master template; Spain and Portugal then inherit it, so each subsequent market is faster and more consistent than the last.

StepMoveWhy now
1Rebuild France on the WiSE standard — French, September-2026 hook, AKAO/PPF surfaced, three-phase roadmap. Extract it as the master template.Largest gap × highest urgency; creates the reusable base.
2Spain — re-front the existing page on the master: lead with the dual clock, consolidate naming, lift SfA above the fold.Closest to standard; editorial effort, fast win, large base.
3Portugal — consolidate the estate behind SfA on the master; retire "TOC," remove "migração," keep the strong SVAT/SAF-T grounding.Mostly consolidation; lowest urgency, so it waits — but inherits everything.
4Bank the master template + rubric as the global pattern for the next markets.Turns three fixes into a repeatable capability.

What this buys the programme

A defensible reason to start with France today, a fast follow in Spain, a tidy consolidation in Portugal — and a tested master template that lets every future Sage market launch a consistent, accurate accountant page in days. The demos show the destination; this review shows why the journey starts where it does.
