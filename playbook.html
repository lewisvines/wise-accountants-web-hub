00 — Orientation

The same template cannot ship three times

Across the three demos there is one strong, repeatable pattern — and three different regulatory truths it is being stretched over. The job of this playbook is to lock the pattern, name the rules that make it work, and give every future market a way to launch fast without losing the things that make it Sage.

Two structural facts shape everything below. First, the regulatory clock is the wedge on every page — but the clock reads differently in each country, and a copy-pasted "2027" claim is already inaccurate in Portugal. Second, there are two design systems quietly competing in the repos: the demo trio's bespoke green (#00DC82 + Fraunces) and sage-freemium-france's Sage-brand green (#00dc06 + forest + "Sage Text"). Before this becomes the global pattern, the family must converge on one tokenised system.

!

The one principle everything else serves

Lead with the customer's regulatory reality — accurately, per country — framed as their opportunity, never as a Sage product pitch and never with an apology. Acknowledge ≠ apologise. Investment-led, customer-voice, forward-facing.

01 — Principles

What belongs on the page — and what doesn't

Derived from the strongest shared pattern in the Spain and Portugal builds, pressure-tested against the tone discipline the WiSE programme already runs on.

✓Include — the winning pattern

An accurate regulatory hook, in the customer's voiceOpen on what changes for their clients, dated to that country's real mandate. "What this means for your firm," not "introducing Sage Active."
Acknowledge-and-reset, without apologyThe ES demo's "we know the message hasn't always been clear" owns past confusion then resets to a confident forward position. Honest, not sorry.
Collapse complexity into one Sage answer"Two clocks, one platform" (ES). Name the competing obligations, then show a single place to handle them.
A clear, phased roadmapMap to the three-phase France logic: live today / on the roadmap / in discovery. Show direction; never present roadmap items as available now.
An honest AI position"Where we're taking AI in the practice" — a stated direction of travel, not vapour, not over-claim.
Transparent pricing as a differentiator"A clear price for every firm size." Clarity is the competitive wedge in a confused market.
Five scannable anchors + repeated specific CTAsClock / platform / journey / AI / pricing. CTAs are channel-appropriate and working: Request a demo · Talk to a specialist · Get started.
Visible proof of compliance standingThe DGFiP-registered PA (AKAO) for France; VeriFactu/Crea y Crece readiness for Spain. Present, not buried.

✕Exclude — the anti-patterns

Apologetic languageNo "sorry," no "we got it wrong," no mea culpa. It undercuts the investment-led posture and the channel's confidence in Sage.
The word "migration"Banned. It frames change as cost and disruption rather than readiness and growth.
A product-led heroDon't open with feature lists or product names. The customer's regulatory reality is the door; the product is what's behind it.
A copy-pasted regulatory clockThe single biggest risk. "In 2027 every client changes how they invoice" is true-ish for FR/ES and wrong for PT. Re-ground per market.
Wrong or missing localisationEvery country page must carry the correct language and lang attribute, currency, date format and local regulator names. Verify the outer shell as well as the content on every build.
Two design systemsPick one tokenised system (brand green + agreed type) and hold it. Bespoke per-market palettes break the family and slow replication.
Bloat & gating on live pagesGating is right for stakeholder review (FR), wrong for acquisition. The 880 KB encrypted payload and password wall can't ship to the public.
Overpromised roadmap & competitor-bashingDon't show Phase 2/3 as live. Don't attack rivals — win on clarity and readiness, in the customer's voice.

06 — The replication system

How to launch any market in days, not weeks

The point of the rubric, the lock and the master template is speed without drift. This is the repeatable sequence for the next country — and the loop that keeps every page improving.

The country-launch sequence

  1. Fork the master templateOne tokenised codebase (PT structure + ES content/tone, on the Sage brand system). Components, not bespoke markup.
  2. Re-ground the regulatory clockEstablish the country's real mandate and dates from primary sources. This is the only section that must be researched fresh every time — and the one most likely to be wrong if copied.
  3. Localise properlyLanguage + correct lang, currency, date format, local regulator and product names, and the country's proof assets.
  4. Apply the terminology lockInherit the master glossary verbatim; add country-specific locked terms.
  5. Score against the rubric before launchTarget ≥ 45/50. Anything under 4 on hook, tone, localisation or terminology blocks the launch.
  6. Ship open & light, gate only for reviewPublic pages: no password, performance budget met, correct meta. Keep the gated build for stakeholder previews only.
  7. Re-score quarterly & feed wins backAnything that lifts one market's score becomes a master-template upgrade every market inherits. That's the global iteration loop.
★

What "good" looks like at scale

Any Sage market can stand up a new accountant page that is on-brand, correctly localised, regulatorily accurate and scores 45+ on the rubric — in days — because the structure, tone, terminology and design tokens are inherited, and only the local regulatory truth is built fresh.
